About Her Life

Born in 1910 as Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhiu in Skopje, Mother Teresa dedicated her life to serving the poorest of the poor. She founded the Missionaries of Charity in 1950, a global organization that continues her mission of compassion and service.

Achievements & Milestones

Mother Teresa received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979 for her extraordinary efforts in alleviating the suffering of the poor and promoting peace worldwide. She humbly accepted it "in the name of the hungry, the naked, the homeless."

In 1950, Mother Teresa established the Missionaries of Charity in Kolkata, a congregation dedicated to serving "the poorest of the poor." This marked the beginning of her global mission of love and service.

India honored Mother Teresa with the Padma Shri in 1962, recognizing her immense contributions to society through her compassionate work with the underprivileged.

Mother Teresa was awarded Indiaâ€™s highest civilian honor, the Bharat Ratna, in 1980 for her humanitarian efforts that touched millions of lives across the nation and beyond.

The United Kingdom bestowed the Order of Merit upon Mother Teresa in 1983, acknowledging her tireless dedication to the service of humanity.

Mother Teresa was granted honorary citizenship of the United States in 1996, one of only a few individuals to receive this rare honor, in recognition of her global humanitarian impact.

On September 4, 2016, Mother Teresa was canonized by Pope Francis as Saint Teresa of Calcutta, celebrating her life of holiness and service to the poor.
